Slate & Silicon

The internet changed how your mind feels.

You probably didn’t notice when it started.

Stay for a minute

At some point, I stopped being alone with my thoughts.

There was always something in between.

A screen.
A notification.
A small interruption I didn’t question.

I started reaching for my phone before I even knew why.

Not to do anything specific.

Just to feel… occupied.

Silence felt off.

Like something was missing.

Or like I was supposed to be doing something else.

I couldn’t sit through things the same way anymore.

Movies.
Conversations.
Even my own thoughts.

My attention didn’t disappear.

It broke into pieces.

I thought I was just tired.

Or distracted.

Or getting worse at focusing.

I wasn’t.

I was adapting.

I thought it was a discipline problem.

It wasn’t.

It was the environment.

One keeps you occupied.

The other lets you return.

Not productivity

Not optimization

Not discipline

Just recovery.

Nothing is wrong with you.

You learned how to function in a system that rewards interruption.

It just never taught you how to come back.

That’s what this is for.
